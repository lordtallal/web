Addressing Urbanisation and Population crisis in Pakistan.

The objective of this research is to examine the population and urbanisation crisis in Pakistan, with a focus on investigating the factors contributing to the rapid rise of urbanisation in select districts. Existing literature on the topic primarily addresses the negative consequences of overpopulation, but overlooks the reasons behind the concentration of population in only a few districts. To address this gap, this research aims to identify the key variables driving the increase in population and urbanisation in Pakistan, and explores the issue of uneven distribution of population and urban areas across the country. The research contends that urbanisation and population growth can be beneficial if they are distributed equitably, but the current situation in Pakistan calls for a closer examination. The research methodology involved integrating data from various sources to perform an analysis and develop a machine learning model to identify the most significant variables related to the research problems.
